1 .B.Tech and BE: Clarity in Terminology

B.Tech stands for Bachelor of Technology, whereas B.E stands for Bachelor of Engineering. There are a plethora of students who are in a dilemma regarding B.E and B.Tech because both these degrees are almost similar, but this is not the case.

Both the Bachelor of Technology and the Bachelor of Engineering degrees have their own distinctions and significance. In terms of study, the main difference between B.Tech and B.E. is that B.Tech focuses on realistic application, whereas B.E. aims to focus on theoretical knowledge applied to practical situations.

3. B.Tech and BE: Eligibility Criteria

Both B.Tech and B.E degrees have most things in common, and the eligibility criteria are almost the same to a great extent. The eligibility criteria for B.Tech and B.E. are:

  • Candidates must have completed their higher secondary education or 12th grade in science streams, with core subjects such as Chemistry, Physics, and Mathematics.
  • Candidates need to score a minimum of 50% marks in 12th grade from a recognised university or institution.
